What is PPODA?

PPODA = Proof of Attempted Delivery Away
When a customer is NOT home at delivery time, you leave a FedEx door tag on their door. Your job is to photograph that door tag in a way that:

  • ✓ Shows the door tag CLEARLY and in focus
  • ✓ Proves it's on THE CORRECT CUSTOMER'S DOOR (context only)
  • ✓ PROTECTS customer privacy (no personal information visible)
  • ✓ Meets FedEx standards for proof of attempt

🔒 PRIVACY IS YOUR #1 PRIORITY

  • FedEx Standard: Minimize capture of unnecessary personal property
  • Your Job: Photograph door tag + minimal context ONLY
  • What's Minimal Context? Just enough of the door/entrance to prove this is the correct delivery location
  • What's Too Much? Address numbers, customer shoes, plants, mailboxes, house decorations, doormats with names, anything that identifies the customer
  • Zero People: Never photograph anyone in PPODA. If someone appears, they're in the wrong photo.

✓ DO's and ✗ DON'Ts for PPODA

✓ DO THIS:
  • Photograph door tag clearly
  • Include door/entrance for context
  • Make door tag 60-70% of photo
  • Ensure date/time is captured
  • Frame tightly on door only
  • Use good lighting
  • Take 2-3 shots for best quality
  • Verify photo is clear before moving on
✗ DON'T DO THIS:
  • Show house/address number
  • Photograph customer items/shoes
  • Capture house decorations/details
  • Include any people in photo
  • Show names on mailbox/doormat
  • Take wide angle shots
  • Photograph windows with people
  • Include yard/landscaping details

◆ FedEx PPOD Privacy Guidelines (What You Must Know)

  • Personal Property: Framing should be minimized to avoid capturing unnecessary personal property on the customer's doorstep or entrance.
  • People: Efforts must be made to avoid capturing images of individuals in your photo.
  • Address Visibility: While some part of the address might be visible, FedEx guidelines aim to limit this as much as possible. Your job is to blur or avoid it completely.
  • Tight Framing: Focus your camera on the door and door tag. Don't show the whole house, yard, or street.
  • Legal Requirement: These aren't just FedEx suggestions—they're privacy protection requirements in many regions.

📱 3-Step PPODA Photography Process

1

POSITION YOUR PHONE

Stand 3-4 feet from the door. Hold phone steady. Frame door tag to fill 60-70% of the photo. Door should take up rest of background.

2

CHECK FOR PRIVACY LEAKS

Before clicking: Is any address visible? Any personal items? Any people? If YES, reframe the photo. Move left, right, or closer until privacy items are OUT of frame.

3

TAKE & VERIFY

Take photo. Review on your phone. Is door tag clear? Is context sufficient? Does it prove location without exposing privacy? If NO, retake. One good photo = 5 seconds. Problem photos = hours of complaints.

🚫 Common PPODA Mistakes to AVOID

❌ MISTAKE #1: Shooting Too Wide

Taking a wide-angle photo showing the entire front of the house, address number, landscaping, and everything visible. This is overkill and exposes the customer's privacy.

✓ FIX: Frame ONLY the door and door tag. Nothing else.

❌ MISTAKE #2: Showing Address/House Number

If house numbers are above the door or on the frame, they might be visible. This is a privacy risk. The door tag itself has the tracking info—you don't need to show the address again.

✓ FIX: Angle camera to exclude house number from frame, or zoom in on door tag.

❌ MISTAKE #3: Customer Items Visible

Shoes, plants, welcome mats, mailboxes with names, or anything that reveals personal information about the resident is visible in photo.

✓ FIX: Step back or to the side. Keep photo tightly framed on door only.

❌ MISTAKE #4: Unclear/Blurry Door Tag

Photo is out of focus, too dark, or door tag text is unreadable. This is worthless as proof and defeats the purpose of PPODA.

✓ FIX: Use phone flashlight if needed. Take multiple shots. Delete blurry ones.

💡 Driver Pro Tips

🔦 LIGHTING: If door is in shadow, use your phone's flashlight or stand to the side where light is natural. Dark, unclear photos are rejected.

📐 FRAMING: Zoom in (use your phone's zoom) to get door tag large and clear. Don't just shoot wide. Door tag should be biggest thing in photo.

📱 MULTIPLE SHOTS: Take 3 photos. Delete the blurry ones. Keep the best. This takes 10 seconds and saves you hours of problems later.

✋ HAND STABILITY: Lean against wall or vehicle for stability. Shaky photos = worthless photos. Take your time.
